    On 15th June 2005 The Right to Information Act 2005 was passed by the Parliament. It comes into force on 12th October 2005. Any citizen of India may request information for a "Public Authority" under the provision of this Act, (A body of Government or 'Instrumentality of State'). It required replying expeditiously or within thirty days. The act also required every public authority to computerize there records for wide dissemination and to proactively certain categories of information so that the citizens need minimum recourse to request for information formally. Lokmanya Tilak Arts, Commerce & Science College, Sevli,has declared as a "Public Authority" under this Act as the implementation of 'The Right to Information Act, 2005. The Act Commands that organisations defined as "Public Authorities" are obliged to make available information to the community and generate required preparations in order to encourage clearness and liability in the working for each public right. According to it some provisions of this act have come into effect instantly on its performance. An officer has been designated as Public Information Officer (PIO) in pursuance of the Right to Information Act 2005.PIO shall perform the duties and responsibilities as predicted under the act without fail. This kind of information shall be presented to the community at large through the website. The structure of RTI and works of PIO are available on the website. The Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005, was enacted by the Indian Parliament on 15th June 2005 and came into effect on 12th October 2005. Under this Act, any citizen of India has the right to request information from a "public authority," which includes government bodies or institutions funded by the government. The public authority is required to provide information promptly, or within thirty days of the request. Lokmanya Tilak College, Sevli, as a recognized public authority, is committed to maintaining transparency and ensuring that relevant information is readily available to the public. As per the RTI Act, our college has designated a Public Information Officer (PIO) to handle requests and queries related to information about the college. The PIO is responsible for providing accurate and timely information to applicants in accordance with the provisions of the RTI Act. Additionally, our First Appellate Authority (FAA) is available to address any appeals regarding RTI responses. Both the PIO and FAA are here to assist the public in accessing information about Lokmanya Tilak College’s activities, facilities, and initiatives. For easy access to information, Lokmanya Tilak College has computerized and published essential details on our official website. The website contains the structure of RTI, PIO responsibilities, and various resources to facilitate the public’s access to information. Visit our website for further details at https://ltcollege.com/.
